The Lifecycle of Stars

Exploring the birth, evolution, and death of stars in the universe

Formation Main Sequence End of Life

Formation of Stars

Stars begin their journey in vast clouds of gas and dust known as nebulae. Under the influence of gravity, these clouds collapse and form a dense core called a protostar. As the core heats up, nuclear fusion ignites, giving birth to a new star.

Main Sequence Stars

During the main sequence phase, stars shine steadily as they fuse hydrogen into helium in their cores. This stage can last billions of years, depending on the star's size. Our Sun is currently in this stage.

The Death of Stars

When stars exhaust their nuclear fuel, their fate depends on their mass. Smaller stars become white dwarfs, while massive stars explode as supernovae, leaving behind neutron stars or black holes.
